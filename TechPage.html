BEFORE WE START

Before your start spending your time and Money on a marker, there are few things you MUST know.

  • ITS NOT A TOY!. playing paintball is a lot of fun!, its a great way to loose a few kilos and make great friends. maybe even some life long friends, i know i made a bunch!. but a Paintball Maker is NOT a TOY. it can cause serious harm if missused. You should never use it outside the playing field where everyone has their safety equipment on.
  • speaking of safety,ALWAYS wear yours!, specially your mask. your mask should never be lifted during a game and you should only remove it from your face where and when its safe.
  • BARREL SOCK. an important safety feture of your marker. use it! it can, and often does, help avoid injuries. A barrel sock goes at the end of your barrel. You should place it on the barrel as soon as you screw in on to your marker.
  • ALWAYS USE CAUTION. Personaly, i treat my marker as a real firearm, and you should too!. Technology on a paintball marer has evolved quite a lot since it inception. The newer models tend to hold air within them to maintain consistency on their performancy, even when you take your Air tank off. That means they can STILL FIRE a shot, you should treat it as LOADED MARKER untill you degass it (see Maintenance of your equipment to lear how).
Markers come in many sizes and forms, all are great fun to use, but ALL should be used with great care.

Your Marker

What is a Paintball marker? The definition of a marker according to wikipedia is:

  • A paintball marker, also known as a paintball gun, paint gun, or marker, is the main piece of paintball equipment in the sport of paintball. Markers use an expanding gas, such as carbon dioxide (CO2) or compressed air, to propel paintballs through the barrel and quickly strike a target. The term "marker" is derived from its original use as a means for forestry personnel to mark trees and ranchers to mark wandering cattle.[1][2][3]
  • The muzzle velocity of paintball markers is approximately 90 m/s (300 ft/s). While greater muzzle velocity is possible, it has been ruled unsafe for use on most commercial paintball fields. [4] When paintballs hit an object at high speed they have the potential to cause damage; a paintball colliding with human skin, even protected by cloth, may cause bruising or further tissue damage. However, the damage depends on the paintball's velocity, its impact angle, whether it breaks, and which part of the body it hits. Because of the potential for serious soft tissue damage, paintball players must wear masks to protect their eyes, mouth, and ears when barrel blocking devices are not preventing paintball markers from firing.
  • Most paintball markers can be disassembled into four main components: the body, hopper, barrel, and gas system or air tank.
